Analysis and Prediction of Crime Hotspots using machine learning with Stacked Generalization Approach

Information

Ensemble learning method is a collaborative decision-making mechanism that implements to aggregate the predictions of learned classifiers in order to produce new instances. Early analysis has shown that the ensemble classifiers are more reliable than any single part classifier, both empirically and logically. While several ensemble methods are presented, it is still not an easy task to find an appropriate configuration for a particular dataset. Several prediction-based theories have been proposed to handle machine learning crime prediction problem in India. It becomes a challenging problem to identify the dynamic nature of crimes. Crime prediction is an attempt to reduce crime rate and deter criminal activities

Algorithms

In this project, we will implement several machine learning algorithms to analyze and predict crime hotspots. These algorithms play a crucial role in processing and interpreting the crime data, enabling us to make informed predictions and take proactive measures to address potential criminal activities.

Decision Tree

Decision trees are a popular machine learning technique used for classification and regression tasks. They partition the feature space into regions, making decisions based on simple rules inferred from the data. Decision trees are intuitive and easy to interpret, making them valuable for understanding the underlying patterns in crime data and identifying important contributing factors.

Random Forest

Random forests are an ensemble learning method that constructs multiple decision trees during training and outputs the mode of the classes (classification) or the mean prediction (regression) of the individual trees. They are robust against overfitting and tend to produce more accurate predictions by aggregating the outputs of multiple trees. Random forests are particularly effective in handling high-dimensional datasets with complex relationships among variables.

Gradient Boosting Classifier

Gradient boosting classifiers are another ensemble learning technique that builds a series of weak learners, typically decision trees, in a sequential manner. Each new learner focuses on the errors made by the previous ones, gradually improving the overall model's performance. Gradient boosting is known for its high predictive accuracy and flexibility in capturing complex relationships in the data. It is widely used in various domains, including crime prediction, due to its superior performance.
